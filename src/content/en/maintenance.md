- The course structures, tutorial entry points, data documentation, and 2026 event statuses in this document were verified on **2026-07-14**; platform access policies, live-session dates, application statuses, and software versions change, and the official pages should always take precedence.
- The "suggested hours" for courses/books are an actionable planning aid, not an official commitment; adjust based on assignment completion, not video-watching progress.
- This roadmap is oriented toward general computational and systems neuroscience. Wet-lab work, animal surgery, human-subjects research, and clinical/medical-device development require formal safety, ethics, and professional training from your institution — online materials cannot substitute for this.
- For books not explicitly marked as having an open full text, obtain them through a publisher, library, or legitimate purchase; author-provided code, exercises, or open chapters do not mean the whole book is openly licensed.
- Only three categories of content need maintenance every 6 months: links to overall course catalogs, software/data API versions, and the current year's summer-school/workshop statuses. Classic textbooks and skill dependencies don't need to chase annual changes.

The final test is simple: if you can explain a phenomenon with a minimal model, test it against real data, honestly show your uncertainty and counterexamples, and let someone else rerun the entire process, you have moved from "studying computational neuroscience" to "doing computational neuroscience."
