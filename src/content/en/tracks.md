### 4.1 24-week fast track (12–18 hours/week)

Suited to people who already have a foundation in Python, linear algebra, calculus, and probability; the goal is "be able to read papers and complete one small project," not to replace systematic training.

| Week | Content |
|---:|---|
| 1–2 | Neuromatch prerequisite diagnostics + Neuro Video Series + LIF project |
| 3–10 | Neuromatch core: model fitting, GLM, PCA, signal processing, LDS, biological neuron models, dynamics, Bayes/HMM/RL |
| 11–16 | Selected chapters of *Neuronal Dynamics*: 1–4, 7, 10–12, 15–19 |
| 17–20 | Real-data analysis with Allen/IBL/DANDI |
| 21–24 | Paper-figure reproduction, robustness, report, and release |

### 4.2 18–24 month deep-dive version

- First complete the full 64-week path.
- Then spend 3–6 months closely reading an advanced textbook and working through its problems — e.g., Izhikevich, Ermentrout & Terman, or Kass/Eden/Brown.
- Then spend 3–6 months on a second data modality/second specialization, contributing to an open-source project, joining a reading group, and applying for summer schools or lab rotations.

### 4.3 Weekly time template

| Activity | Time | Principle |
|---|---:|---|
| Videos/lecture notes | 2 h | Only note down questions, assumptions, and key derivations |
| Textbook/papers | 2 h | Derive actively; don't aim to read cover-to-cover |
| Coding/assignments | 4–5 h | Implement from a blank slate; build baselines and checks |
| Review/writing | 1 h | One-page summary or one oral explanation |
| Project maintenance/discussion | 1–2 h | Git, issues, code review, reading group |
